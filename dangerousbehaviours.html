Dangerous Behaviours when Driving

Mobile Phone Use

There seems to be public confusion regarding the dos and don'ts of laws surrounding mobile phones and the road. A study in Queensland found 42% of drivers had broken these laws demonstrating an unawareness surrounding these laws. On an unrestricted license you can use a mobile phone to answer calls through audio functions or bluetooth given your phone is in the cradle. Phones can also be used for navigation given the phone is in a fixed cradle. Digital payment services can be used if you are are stationary and located off the road. Outside from these circumstances it is illegal to use your phone if you are a provisional or learner driver, or your vehicle is not stationary and away from the road.

Drugs and Alcohol

Upon taking drugs (illegal or legal) or Alcohol the risks of an injury and a crash are increased. Hence, consumption of drugs or alcohol (alcohol in amounts above 0.05 blood alcohol concentration) is unwise. In fact when blood alcohol levels exceed 0.05 the casualty risk when driving doubles and the risk of involvement in a crash sharply increases. Since 2017, in 37% of all fatalities involving drivers or motorcyclists the victims had drugs in their system. So you are all round increasing the likelihood of fatalities not to mention a potential sentence of 14 years if you have a crash whilst under the influence of drugs or alcohol. Not to mention for learners and provisional license drivers there is a zero tolerance policy for drugs and alcohol.

Fatigue

Driving whilst tired can have similar affects to driving whilst under the influence. If you have not slept in 17 hours the affect is similar to a blood alcohol level of 0.05. Sleeping for 4-5 hours in the past 24 hours increases the chance of a crash increases by 4.5 times or more when compared to a driver with seven hours sleep. With just four hours of sleep there is a 11.5 times increase in the likelihood of a crash.
